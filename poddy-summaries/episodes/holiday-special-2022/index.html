In brief

Acquired's 2022 retrospective uses the year's episodes and technology shocks to refine a theory of durable media and business strategy. Taylor Swift demonstrates internet-scale demand, NVIDIA demonstrates repeated company-saving technical bets, Walmart shows technology must improve financial structure, and Benchmark shows that a distinctive format comes from trade-offs rather than merely working harder.

The hosts also confront the cost of credibility after interviewing Sam Bankman-Fried, explain why they answered FTX through evergreen Enron history instead of real-time commentary, and assess the first ChatGPT moment with excitement but uncertainty. Their conclusion about Acquired is equally disciplined: remain small, infrequent, long-form, selective, and willing to reject attractive opportunities that weaken the product's differentiated promise.

Five key insights

  1. The internet enlarges hits and nichesTaylor Swift generated an estimated 900 stadiums of Ticketmaster demand, while specialist products such as Stratechery and Acquired can reach unexpectedly large global audiences. Internet distribution simultaneously supports far more creators and concentrates unprecedented attention around the biggest stars.
  2. Credibility creates responsibility after failureThe hosts acknowledge that featuring Bankman-Fried may have helped legitimize FTX for listeners, even though they could not have known about the fraud. Their response was to apologize directly and produce an Enron history that supplied durable patterns without pretending to offer definitive real-time reporting.
  3. Technology must change business economicsCalling a company tech-enabled is meaningful only when technology reduces a previously large cost or expands distribution on the same cost base. Walmart qualifies because logistics coordination enabled structurally lower prices and greater volume; superficial software layered onto unchanged economics does not.
  4. Strategy is a chosen set of trade-offsDoing more research than other interviewers is operational excellence, not strategy. Acquired's strategy is publishing infrequently, making unusually long episodes, staying a two-person operation, and earning enough trust to combine a deeply researched history with access to its protagonists.
  5. ChatGPT revealed capability before applicationsDALL-E 2, GPT-3, and stateful ChatGPT made a computing shift visible before the hosts could name its largest market. They compare the moment to the early App Store: obvious platform power, many small first-order ideas, and only a few entrepreneurs capable of seeing the defining applications.
