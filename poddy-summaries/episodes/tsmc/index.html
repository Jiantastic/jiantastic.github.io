In brief

Morris Chang founded TSMC at 56 after wars, immigration, failed MIT qualifying exams, and a brilliant but stalled Texas Instruments career. His key manufacturing insight was that high initial volume accelerates yield learning, which lowers cost and improves performance. Taiwan supplied half the capital and a national industrial mandate; Philips supplied 28% and equipment ties. The pure-play foundry initially had no real market, but it removed the fab cost that prevented chip designers from leaving incumbents and starting companies.

Qualcomm, Broadcom, Nvidia, AMD, Apple, and hyperscalers turned that possibility into a compounding platform. Neutrality builds trust because TSMC never designs competing products; aggregate customer volume funds leading-edge fabs and ASML tools; every node improves customer products and attracts more demand. In the 2025 remaster, TSMC is the essential manufacturer for AI and Apple silicon, worth over $1 trillion. Its moat combines scale and irreplicable process learning, but concentrates the world's advanced compute capacity in geopolitically exposed Taiwan.

Five key insights

  1. Volume turns manufacturing into accumulated knowledgeSemiconductor yields begin low and improve through repeated production, measurement, and process adjustment. Pricing aggressively to fill a new fab sacrifices early margin but accelerates learning. The resulting yield advantage compounds into lower costs, better reliability, more customers, and still more data competitors cannot purchase instantly.
  2. Neutrality created the fabless industryAn integrated manufacturer might prioritize its own chips or learn from a customer's design. TSMC promised not to compete, letting engineers form companies with tens of millions rather than billions of dollars. It became the shared manufacturing platform beneath Nvidia, Qualcomm, Broadcom, AMD, and countless startups.
  3. The foundry flywheel funds the next nodeCustomer growth produces high-margin cash, which funds newer fabs and scarce ASML equipment. Better process technology improves every customer's power, performance, and addressable market, creating more volume. As Rock's Law raises fab costs, only the largest platform can keep paying to stay on Moore's Law.
  4. Apple validated strategic trust at enormous scaleApple needed to escape Samsung, its manufacturing supplier and smartphone rival. TSMC committed $9 billion and 6,000 employees to dedicated capacity without a backup plan on either side. The iPhone relationship converted pure-play neutrality from an industry principle into one of technology's largest partnerships.
  5. Geography is the unhedgeable concentration riskMoney can build equipment and buildings elsewhere, but not instantly transplant supplier coordination, engineering culture, yield history, or tacit factory knowledge. Taiwan's political status makes TSMC simultaneously an economic shield, a strategic target, and a single point of failure for advanced consumer, cloud, defense, and AI systems.
